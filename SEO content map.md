### [COMPONENT] Trend Window FAQ — answers must be in crawlable DOM

**Q1: Why is building in Web3 and AI hard?**
Web3 and AI move in windows, not roadmaps. A new chain, a new agent paradigm, or a regulatory shift opens an output window that closes within weeks. Most teams need months to assemble, agree, and ship. By the time they are ready to build, the window has already closed. The problem is not the technology — it is the team structure. Fragmented ownership across vendors, phases, and contractors is what kills speed.

**Q2: How fast does Metaborong ship?**
We target production delivery inside the open trend window: typically week three to week six from specification to shipped code. Speed comes from one senior team that owns architecture, engineering, and deployment continuously. We do not re-bid talent across phases or hand off across vendor boundaries. AbsolveMe needed its launch site live before a liquidity window closed — we shipped site, content, and design support in two days, followed by the Solana–NEAR cross-chain layer in five more.

**Q3: Why do agencies and freelancers miss trend windows?**
Large agencies optimise for multi-quarter, scope-locked engagements. They ship months after the window closes. Freelance teams ship quickly but fragment ownership across roles, producing code that breaks once real user load and edge cases arrive. Trend-driven Web3 and AI products need both speed and architectural durability — a combination that requires a senior, cohesive team working under one roof.

**Q4: What is a trend window in Web3 and AI?**
A trend window is the period between a market shift opening and that same shift dissolving. In Web3, examples include new chain launches, token standard changes, and regulatory shifts. In AI, they include new model capability releases and agent paradigm shifts. These windows typically last four to eight weeks. Products that ship after the window closes face a different market than the one they were specified for.

**Q5: How long does it take to ship a Web3 MVP?**
A Web3 MVP — wallet integration, smart-contract logic, and a working frontend — ships in three to six weeks when one senior team owns architecture, engineering, and deployment continuously. Longer timelines typically point to fragmented ownership across vendors, or specifications that keep shifting mid-build. We scope every engagement in a single discovery sprint before a line of code is written.

**FAQ Q&A for /services:**

**Q: How do you price your services?**
Most engagements are fixed-bid after a scoped discovery sprint. We do not bill time and materials because open-ended invoicing misaligns incentives — it rewards slowness. The discovery sprint is priced separately and gives you a precise build cost before you commit to the full project. Retainer engagements after launch are monthly flat-rate.

**Q: Do you take equity?**
Occasionally, for the right early-stage founders. Our equity involvement at SunsetML began as a standard build engagement and evolved into co-foundership after the first build phase. We are selective — equity arrangements work when there is a genuine long-term alignment, not as a substitute for a build budget.

**Q: Can you work with our existing team?**
Yes. We operate well as an embedded external team alongside existing in-house engineers or other contractors. We require one clear technical decision-maker on the client side and a shared project space. We do not operate well in committee-driven environments where architecture decisions require multiple approval layers.

**Q: What happens after the build is done?**
We offer a post-launch Operate retainer for clients who want to keep the same team on the product. For clients who prefer to hand off to an in-house team, we produce full documentation and a structured knowledge transfer sprint before offboarding. We do not disappear after deployment.

We architect products with SOC 2 in mind — access controls, audit logging, encryption at rest and in transit, and vendor management practices — but we do not directly manage the certification process. We work alongside your compliance team or a specialist audit firm.
