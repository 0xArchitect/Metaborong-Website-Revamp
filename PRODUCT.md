# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Web3, AI, and SaaS founders evaluating a senior development studio to build something
hard. They are technical or technical-adjacent: they can smell a thin agency in seconds
and they have been burned by ticket-shufflers before. Their context when they arrive:
comparing options under real stakes (a protocol, an AI product, an MVP with a runway
clock), deciding whether this team is worth a discovery call. Primary geographies are the
US and Europe; the buyer is usually a founder or technical lead, not a procurement
department.

The job to be done: in the first minute, decide "these people actually ship hard things"
and find the proof fast (services, work, who's behind it) without wading through marketing.

## Product Purpose

Metaborong (metaborong.com) is the marketing site for a boutique studio doing Web3
protocol work, AI agent development, and product engineering. It exists to convert
evaluating founders into discovery calls by demonstrating senior engineering credibility,
and to rank/get cited for service-intent and answer-engine queries (SEO/AEO/GEO) where
larger competitors are thin or absent. Web3 and AI carry equal weight; the site is never
framed as Web3-first.

Success: a qualified founder reads two or three sections, believes the team is real, and
books a call. Secondarily, the service-leaf cluster earns organic and AI-citation traffic
the current site (DA 2, ~0 organic) does not.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **engineered, deliberate, early.** The voice is direct, confident, and
technically precise. Every claim is verifiable; no marketing inflation ("revolutionary",
"game-changing", "best-in-class" are banned). Copy speaks to engineers, not to
procurement. "Designed, not assembled" is the posture: every line has a structural job.

Emotional target, in the first five seconds: a fusion of **calm premium confidence** (quiet,
deliberate, expensive restraint, in control) and **sharp modernity** (the disciplined team
that is genuinely early on Web3 + AI without the hype). Not loud, not safe. Boutique
substance over agency polish.

## Anti-references

The site must explicitly NOT feel like any of these:

- **Generic Web3 agency** (SoluLab, Antier and that crowd): neon-on-black, glow gradients,
  floating 3D blobs, "revolutionary blockchain solutions." The dominant reflex to avoid.
- **Enterprise consultancy beige** (Accenture/Deloitte register): stock-photo corporate,
  safe navy, procurement-speak, zero personality.
- **AI-startup SaaS template**: purple-gradient hero, default Inter, identical
  feature-card grids, the interchangeable Vercel-clone look.
- **Loud crypto-bro maximalism**: aggressive animation, meme energy, hype language,
  "degens welcome."

Two structural anti-patterns from prior work: card-only monotony (identical icon +
heading + text grids repeated down the page) and any layout that reads as assembled from
a template rather than designed.

## Design Principles

- **Show the engineering, don't claim it.** Credibility comes from structure, precision,
  and one real signature visual per section, not from adjectives.
- **Restraint is the flex.** Quiet, deliberate, premium. The confidence is in what's left
  out. Motion happens once and stops.
- **Equal pillars.** Web3 and AI are presented with equal weight; never Web3-first, never
  AI-as-afterthought. Product Studio is the third equal voice.
- **Citable by humans and machines.** Every section earns its place for a reading founder
  and for an answer engine; substance is the SEO/AEO strategy.
- **Designed, not assembled.** Reject template reflexes; vary rhythm; every element traces
  to a structural job.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA across all interactive surfaces. Keyboard-first: every interactive element
reachable and operable by keyboard, tab order following visual reading order.
`:focus-visible` rings mandatory. Body copy meets AA contrast (>=4.5:1); the tertiary
`#999` text token never carries body copy. `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` short-circuits
every non-essential animation. Touch targets meet the >=44px tap-target rule (a known
deferral exists on a few nav/footer link hit-areas, tracked separately).
